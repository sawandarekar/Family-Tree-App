Why It Matters

Your story didn't start with you.

Every family has a story stretching back generations — names, dates, places, and connections that define who you are. Without a system to capture them, they fade away.

🏛️

Preserve History

Oral traditions get lost. Physical documents decay. Family Tree keeps your family's history alive in a structured, portable format that survives generations.

🔗

Understand Connections

See how relatives are linked across generations at a glance. Discover shared ancestors, map cousins, and understand the full scope of your family network visually.

🌍

Cultural Identity

Knowing where you come from shapes who you are. Documenting your lineage strengthens your cultural identity and gives the next generation a foundation to stand on.

🧬

Medical Heritage

Family health history matters. Tracking ancestors helps identify inherited conditions and gives your family and doctors crucial context for better health decisions.

❤️

Emotional Legacy

Stories about grandparents and great-grandparents bring families closer. Family Tree gives you a place to attach notes, milestones, and memories to every person.

📦

Yours Forever

No subscriptions. No data lock-in. Your entire family tree is stored locally as a plain JSON file — readable, exportable, and owned by you, always.

How It Helps

From zero to full tree in minutes.

Family Tree is designed to be simple enough to start immediately and powerful enough to grow with your research over years.

1

Add Your First Person

Click "Add Person", enter their name, dates, and any notes you know. A node appears instantly on the canvas — no setup, no form overload.

2

Connect the Dots

Draw edges between nodes to create parent-child relationships. The tree builds itself visually as you connect — clear and intuitive.

3

Let Auto-Layout Do the Work

Hit Auto-Layout and watch your tree snap into a clean, hierarchical structure automatically using the dagre algorithm.

4

Save, Export & Share

Your tree auto-saves locally every time you make a change. Export it as a JSON file to share with family or back it up anywhere.

5

Import & Collaborate

Receive a tree JSON from a relative, import it in one click, and continue building together — no account or sync required.
